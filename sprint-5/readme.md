🛡️ Sprint 05: Incident Response Planning & Playbook Development
Objective: To overhaul and modernize the Incident Response Plan (IRP) for "Capybara Unlimited," a rapidly growing exotic pet enterprise. This project involved auditing a flawed existing plan and aligning it with NIST SP 800-61 best practices to ensure business continuity during cyber-attacks.

🛠️ Strategic Improvements
IRP Audit & Correction: Identified and remediated "bad advice" in the legacy plan, ensuring all procedures followed industry-standard incident handling lifecycles.

Role & Responsibility Matrix: Defined a clear Incident Response Team (IRT) structure, removing non-essential roles and assigning correct technical and administrative ownership.

NIST Lifecycle Alignment: Rebuilt the response framework around the four phases: Preparation; Detection & Analysis; Containment, Eradication, & Recovery; and Post-Incident Activity.

KPI & Metric Definition: Established Key Performance Indicators (e.g., Mean Time to Detect, Mean Time to Respond) to provide stakeholders with measurable data on security effectiveness.

Communication & Compliance: Drafted a communication plan to handle internal technical updates and external regulatory reporting requirements.

🕹️ Specialized Response Playbooks
Developed three distinct, step-by-step playbooks to handle high-probability threats:

Malware Infection: Focused on rapid isolation to prevent lateral movement across internal channels.

Phishing Mitigation: Standardized end-user communication and credential harvesting containment.

DDoS Defense: Outlined traffic pattern differentiation to block malicious requests while maintaining service for legitimate customers.

📄 Project Deliverables
Capybara_Unlimited_IRP_Final.pdf: The complete, corrected Incident Response Plan.

Cyber_Incident_Playbooks.pdf: The technical "cheat sheets" for Malware, Phishing, and DDoS response.

Frameworks: NIST SP 800-61 (Incident Handling Guide)
