🎯 Sprint 04: Strategic Threat Modeling & Risk Assessment
Objective: To develop a comprehensive Threat Model Report for a high-value infrastructure, identifying likely threat actors, mapping attack surfaces, and proposing prioritized defense-in-depth strategies.

🛠️ The Threat Modeling Process
Threat Actor Identification: Profiled the top three most likely threat actors (e.g., Nation-State, Cyber-Criminal, Insider Threat) and detailed their specific motivations and capabilities.

Attack Surface Mapping: Enumerated physical, software, and network entry points to identify the organization's total exposure.

Scenario Analysis: Crafted detailed attack scenarios to visualize how various Attack Vectors could be exploited to reach Key Assets.

Risk Categorization: Evaluated risks based on the probability of occurrence versus the potential business impact.

Proactive Defense Planning: Identified and justified high-impact solutions, including:

Honeypots: To misdirect and identify active intruders.

Detection Strategies: Implementing SIEM rules and behavioral analytics.

Mitigations: Hardening systems via patching, encryption, and access controls.

📄 Project Deliverables
Threat_Model_Worksheet.csv: A detailed matrix of assets, actors, vectors, and risk scores.

Executive_Summary_Report.pdf: A high-level briefing designed for Stakeholders, translating technical threats into business risks and goals.

🧰 Skills & Frameworks
Concepts: Threat Modeling, Attack Surface Reduction, Defense-in-Depth.

Analysis: Attack Vector Analysis, Risk Assessment, Scenario Scripting.

Soft Skills: Executive Communication, Stakeholder Alignment, Strategic Planning.
